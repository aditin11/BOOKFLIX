48 Laws of Power

Robert Greene

Discusses power dynamics, strategy, and social influence through history.
48 Laws of Power examines power dynamics through historical examples. Robert Greene outlines strategies used by influential figures to gain and maintain power. Each law is supported by stories and analysis. The book focuses on human behavior, manipulation, and strategy. While controversial, it provides insight into how power operates in politics, business, and relationships. Readers are encouraged to understand power rather than blindly pursue it. The book promotes awareness and strategic thinking. 48 Laws of Power is best read critically, serving as a guide to recognizing power structures in real life.
